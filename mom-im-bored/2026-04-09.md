## What I Built

Big visual and UX day for Mom, I'm Boerd. Started by completely rethinking the crumpled paper balls that fill the jar. The old ones looked like geometric rubber band balls — flat-shaded triangles clipped to a perfect circle. The new version uses an irregular bumpy silhouette with 18 perimeter points connected by bezier curves, a 3-ring mesh with 56 triangles, height-aware shading that makes ridges catch light and valleys darken, and a layered crease system with deep valley folds, medium curved surface creases, and fine hairline wrinkles. The jar now has 8 balls (up from 5) with low gravity so they float gently instead of slamming to the bottom, and smaller jars on splash/welcome screens get 5 appropriately-sized balls.

The biggest new feature is the Boredom Buddy avatar system. Kids can now choose from six Canvas-drawn animal avatars — each with their own fixed natural colors and a unique personality. There's Rufus the golden retriever (adventurous, always ready to fetch fun), Whiskers the cat (clever mastermind behind puzzles and crafts), Bubbles the jellyfish (dreamy and creative), Scout the fox (born explorer), Mango the monkey (can't sit still, loves being goofy), and Ziggy the snake (sneaky-good at science experiments). Each buddy has a profile sheet with their bio that kids can read. The color picker only changes the background ring color — the animals always look like themselves.

Also streamlined kid switching on the home screen. Previously it was: tap avatar → bottom sheet slides up → pick kid → full-screen social context prompt. Now it's just: tap avatar → dropdown appears right below it → pick kid → done. The social context segment is already in the header bar so there's no need to ask again.

Other polish: activity text consistency pass across all 645 activities, redesigned the "More Like This" sheet with tap-to-expand previews, unified all settings sub-screens with consistent WavyBackground and visual design, and made the Edit Kid screen scrollable with a compact grade level row (removed icons, colored text instead).

## What's Next

Continuing to refine the avatar illustrations — they're functional but could use another polish pass to really sell the cute factor at small sizes. Also want to look at the onboarding flow to make sure the buddy selection feels magical for first-time users.
