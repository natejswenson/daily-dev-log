## What I Built

Today was a big quality and polish push across the entire app. I started at the data layer — auditing and improving the accuracy of all 665 activities in the database, then doing a deep content review of the remaining 371 that needed attention. The goal was making sure every activity suggestion actually makes sense and has solid descriptions.

From there I moved into the UI. I did a full UI/UX polish pass touching 27 improvements across 16 files — tightening up spacing, consistency, and small interaction details that add up. Then I redesigned the detail screen with updated road trip branding, a splash animation on open, and navigation fixes to make the flow feel more intentional.

The big new feature was activity sharing. I built out V1 of native sharing with a branded share card rendered in portrait orientation, pretty URL slugs for each activity, and integration with the system share sheet. I also fixed how `LPLinkMetadata` picks up the pretty slug URLs so link previews look right.

On the reliability side, I resolved 15 bugs surfaced from a codebase audit and added 57 unit tests covering core models, utilities, and extensions. I also fixed the materials scroll behavior on the detail screen, added a "similar activities" section, and enforced a 7-day history limit to keep the experience fresh.

## What's Next

With sharing live and the detail screen redesigned, the next step is likely testing the share links end-to-end in production and iterating on the link preview metadata. The test coverage boost also sets up a good foundation for moving faster on new features.
