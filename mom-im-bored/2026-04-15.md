## What I Built

Killed the three-tier tip structure. After the App Store rejection cycle earlier this week — where the large tip's metadata got flagged for putting price in description text — it was clear the multi-tier setup was carrying more risk than it was worth: three IAP products to keep in sync between Xcode, App Store Connect, and the local `.storekit` file, three localization strings to keep Apple-compliant, three UI states to maintain in the Tip Jar sheet.

For a free-with-tips app, a single tip amount is the cleaner answer. Picked $4.99 as the middle of the prior range — high enough to feel like a meaningful "thanks," low enough that nobody hesitates over a coffee-priced tip. Stripped the small/medium/large branching out of the Tip Jar view, removed two products from `productIds`, deleted the corresponding entries from the `.storekit` config, and simplified the subtitle/icon switch statement down to a single product path.

Net result: less code, less to break, less for the App Store reviewer to scrutinize.

## What's Next

Wait for the new IAP product to clear ASC, then run a fresh App Review submission. After that, the next move is probably analytics — adding lightweight tip-funnel telemetry so I can actually tell whether collapsing to a single tier helped or hurt conversion.
