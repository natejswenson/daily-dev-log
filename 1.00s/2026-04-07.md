## What I Built

The loop mode level map had a background problem — a 1080×12900px JPEG that looked muddy on Retina screens. I ripped it out entirely and replaced it with a fully procedural rendering system that's sharp at any zoom level and responds to player progression (unlocked sections glow brighter than locked ones).

The new background stacks 11 layers: a warm indigo gradient base, an organic hex-cell grid that fades near the edges, full-width section-colored radial washes, a 700-star field with 55 bright feature stars that have cross-flare effects and color-tint near section nodes, Canvas-drawn organic nebula clusters (12 overlapping blobs per node with 120 dust particles each), arena glow halos, interstitial fill clouds between nodes, boss ring echoes with glow backdrops, vertical dust columns, diagonal light rays anchored to section positions, circuit-trace dashed paths paralleling the energy conduits, and left/right edge vignettes. It went through several rounds of opacity boosting — my initial values (0.02-0.08) were practically invisible on device, and the user rightly called it out.

I also replaced the old floating-card confirmation popup (the heavy card with CANCEL/START buttons and a dark overlay) with lightweight glass-card panels that sit directly below the selected orb. They have a connecting line, LEVEL N header, type badge, tap count dots, and a glowing START capsule — much cleaner and more intentional than the old design.

Then I ran 50 iterations of UI/UX polish across the entire map. Locked orbs got bigger (62pt→70pt) so they're actually visible, section labels got progress bars instead of text counters, energy conduits got brighter and wider, boss gates show two-line labels with shadows, the initial scroll now centers on the next-to-play level instead of the section start, and the PLAY button at the bottom now matches the current section's color with a glow treatment. I also removed the redundant level bar overlay — the map speaks for itself.

## What's Next

The background is trending in the right direction but could still use more refinement. The user has been iterating on visual polish aggressively and will likely want more passes on the map and potentially the confirmation panel interaction. There's also the matter of merging this feature branch back to dev.
