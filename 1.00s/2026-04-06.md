## What I Built

Today was all about making each level type feel distinct and polished. I went through six different level mechanics and gave each one a visual and gameplay redesign.

Started with **energy conduit paths** — these replace the old bottom strip with animated energy lines that give the player a clearer sense of the path they're following. The strip got a polish pass too.

Then I tackled the **dual orb** level type. The old version felt cluttered, so I rebuilt it as a slow orbital alignment mechanic where two orbs drift into position. Much more intentional and satisfying to hit.

The **blind zone** and **ghost orb** mechanics got a full redesign. Blind zone now has a cleaner visual language, and ghost orb plays better with the split ring variant. I also fixed a level progress tracking bug that had been lingering.

Did another pass on the same set — **blind zone, ghost orb, and twin strike** — refining the interactions and making sure level progress updates correctly across all three.

Redesigned the **pendulum** to use an arc-swing motion instead of the old linear sweep. Added ghost orb UX improvements alongside it so the two mechanics play well together.

Finished the day with **minimalist level orbs** — each orb now has a type-colored glow with an accent ring. Clean, readable, and immediately tells you what kind of level you're about to play.

## What's Next

With the level type visuals locked in, the next step is playtesting the full progression to make sure difficulty curves feel right across all the redesigned mechanics.
