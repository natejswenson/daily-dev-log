## What I Built

Today was a full UX/UI polish sprint inspired by three games: Flappy Bird's simplicity, Super Hexagon's escalating intensity, and Wordle's competitive hook. The goal was to make 1.00s the most engaging, easy-to-use app on the market.

The biggest architectural change was making the game screen the home screen. No more lobby, no more splash animation for returning users. You tap the app icon and you're looking at a breathing orb — tap it and you're playing. First-ever launch gets a 1-second brand flash, then straight to the orb. The emotional arc is zen calm → intense chaos.

I built a procedural audio engine using AVAudioEngine that synthesizes crystal pings on every hit (sine wave with harmonic shimmer and reverb tail), a low thud on death (the crystal goes silent — absence is the punishment), and richer chord resonance on milestone chains. No bundled audio files — everything is generated mathematically.

The death screen now serves double duty as the competitive hook. When you die at depth 5+, instead of a 2-second auto-dismiss summary, you get your score rising from the ring, your rank with delta, and a mini leaderboard neighborhood (2 above you, YOU highlighted, 2 below). It stays until you tap — you control the pace. The "2 away from @player" line is the one-more-try trigger.

Other polish: the dot now starts orbiting at 2 seconds per revolution and gradually accelerates to 1 second as the window tightens. The background shifts from warm navy/indigo (inviting) to deep purple to near-black as depth increases. The idle breathing animation is slower and more dramatic — the orb genuinely feels alive.

Also added a leaderboard sheet accessible via a chart icon in the corner, with the current user's row highlighted in cyan.

## What's Next

Need to test the crystal audio on a real device for latency. The procedural synthesis should be fine on modern hardware but want to verify the reverb unit doesn't add noticeable delay. Also want to tune the background color progression — the purple/blue shift might need tweaking once I see it on device at extreme depths.
