## What I Built

The image cards had drifted. Each type grew its own look over time, so a feed of them didn't read as one brand. v0.4.0 pulls the whole family onto a single clean, minimal base. The date card is now a realistic ADMIT-ONE ticket. The flow card threads its stages onto a numbered spine. The ramp card became an actual analytics chart, with a trend line over the bars and a growth delta. The STEM card dropped its confetti and saturated gradient but kept the chunky toy blocks. They sit on the same dark canvas now, with the same restraint on color.

Two new card types landed. The code card renders a snippet as a terminal coding session, with line numbers and syntax colors pulled straight from the theme. The Claude Code card is a variant of it: a transcript of a session, the request and what Claude did with it, in Claude's clay accent, for "here's what I shipped with Claude Code" posts.

The carousel got the most work, and it's the follow-through on something I flagged last release. In v0.3 I said the next step was portrait sizing to claim more vertical space; this is that. Carousels are the highest-reach format on LinkedIn, so before touching the design I read up on what actually performs, then rebuilt it as portrait 4:5. Every slide now carries a progress bar and a counter, so a reader can see how far they have to go. The cover stands apart from the content slides, and the deck ends on one clear ask instead of several.

Before opening the PR I ran the changes through a quality gate, fixed the doc issues it surfaced, and merged it as v0.4.0 once CI was green.

## What's Next

The portrait sizing and the progress bar are worth carrying back into the single-image formats. And with the family this consistent, new card types get cheaper to add, since none of them have to reinvent the look.
