## What I Built

Gave the ghostwriter a new way to show momentum. The **ramp card** renders three ascending steps with the final one highlighted — the right shape for a post about an accelerating progression (growth curves, adoption, a streak that's compounding). The bars are deliberately illustrative rather than to-scale; the labeled figures carry the truth, so the visual reads as honest at a glance instead of implying precision it doesn't have.

It's a new card template plus its `.card.ramp` styles, wired into the skill's Visuals guidance so the model knows when to reach for it. The feature had been stranded on an unmerged branch from before the skills were consolidated; I brought it into the monorepo cleanly through a pull request, with the skill's scorer and full test suite green on the way in.

## What's Next

Roll the uncroppable split-row signature out to the other card types if it holds up on more posts, and keep pressure-testing card proportions on real content.

## Update — 08:13 PM

Shipped a second card type: a **STEM card** for education and outreach posts, styled more playful than the existing premium-dark cards — a toy-block S T E M motif in four colors, slight hand-placed rotation, and a rounded hero headline. It reads as kid-energy rather than buttoned-up, which none of the other types do.

The interesting part was attribution. A bottom-corner byline is trivial to crop off, and a tiled watermark looked terrible over the design. The fix: split the eyebrow row into two halves and put the avatar plus LinkedIn handle on the right, sharing a line with real content. Now there's no crop that removes the signature without also cutting the headline. Shipped as 0.2.0 with the template, the `.card.stem` styles, and a SKILL.md reference (committed locally, not yet pushed, so no link).

I also ran the skill end to end for the first time — drafting and publishing a real post (a STEM story about my nephew) with the new card attached. That live run is what surfaced the signature approach and a round of fluff-word trimming, both now captured in the skill's voice notes.
