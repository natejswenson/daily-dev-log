## What Shipped

I asked the coach how my plan was going and two things were wrong. Today's run, already synced from my watch, showed up as "pending." And Saturday, when I took a long recovery walk instead of a run, showed up as a missed day. Both were the grading being too literal, and both were quick to find once I looked.

The "pending" bug came from how the grader handled fresh data. It held any day at or after the most recent synced day as pending, so it wouldn't wrongly call a day missed before the data arrived. Sensible, except that most recent day is today, so today was always pending even with a finished run sitting in the database. The fix flips the logic from date-based to outcome-based: grade the day first, and hold pending only when the verdict is negative and the day isn't done yet. A completed day grades right away. A rest day reads compliant instead of lingering. A half-finished run stays pending rather than booking half credit and quietly healing later, which a review round caught before it shipped.

The walk was the other half. The grader counted running distance only, so a 3.9 mile walk at a 94 average heart rate, which is exactly what a recovery week asks for, scored as a miss. Now easy and recovery days count walking too; the long and quality days stay running-only, because there a walk genuinely isn't the work. Every day also surfaces what you actually did, so the row reads "walked 3.9mi" rather than a blank.

The most useful catch came from the review, not the tests. The backend was clean and unit-tested, but a tightened second-look review flagged that the plan tab colored each row by recomputing a pace miss, independent of the verdict. A walk counts as done now, but walking pace is far slower than a run target, so that done day would have rendered red. The tab now colors from the verdict itself. I confirmed it with a screenshot: Saturday's walk shows green at a 17 minute mile, today grades done, and adherence reads 100 percent.

## What's Next

The grading is honest now; the natural follow-on is letting the coach fold a recovery walk into its read of the week, not just score it.
