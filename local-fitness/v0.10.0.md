## What Shipped

The brief had one voice. Supportive when things were going well, a roast when I
was slipping. That blend is mine, it was baked into the prompt, and it was the
only option. So I made the voice a profile you choose.

There are four. Supportive is your biggest believer; it frames every read as a
bounce-back and never tears you down. Neutral takes the emotion out and tells you
how it is against your goals. Hardass is cynical and never satisfied; anything
short of overachieving gets called out, and it always pushes for more. Adaptive,
the default, is the old behavior unchanged, so a fresh clone gets exactly what it
got before. Each one is a real file you can read and edit, with a written-out
voice and a set of numbers, and you switch between them with a config command.

The hard part, and the thing the review pushed me on, was whether numbers attached
to a tone do anything at all. A coach reads "rip you apart" from the prose and may
just ignore a "harshness: 9" sitting next to it. Numbers that can't be falsified
are decoration. So the thresholds carry real weight: for the parts of the brief
with a concrete goal, like steps and plan adherence, the profile deterministically
decides whether the harsh instruction block goes into the prompt at all. A harsh
profile assembles it, a soft one leaves it out, and the prose voice handles the
rest. That is a switch in code, not a hope about the model, and a test fails if it
breaks.

The other half was testing it the way I keep saying I want to: against expectations,
not by reading a sample. A new scorer runs all four profiles and asserts each keeps
the output schema, the tone categories, and the jargon translation, and that the
harsh-block switch is correct for that profile. It runs in the suite, so it gates
every change. The brief also generates under each profile on demand, and the
default's cross-model run came back consistent. The point was to make the tone a
thing the suite can verify, not a vibe I sign off on.

## What's Next

The profiles are fixed presets today; the natural next step is letting the saved
notes nudge a chosen profile on a specific point, since the precedence for that is
already written into the prompt.
